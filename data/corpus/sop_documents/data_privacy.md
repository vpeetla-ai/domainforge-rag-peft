# Data Privacy

## Scope
Covers customer requests about their personal data: what is held, access
copies, correction, deletion, marketing preferences, and concerns about how
data is used.

## Core Principles
Collect only what is needed, use it only for stated purposes, and never share
customer data with unauthorized parties. Agents access customer records only
to resolve the customer's own request.

## Data Subject Requests
- **Access:** the customer may request a copy of their personal data.
  Fulfilled within the statutory window after identity verification.
- **Correction:** inaccurate details are updated through the verified-change
  workflow.
- **Deletion:** account and personal data are deleted on request, except
  records that must be retained for legal, tax, or fraud-prevention reasons;
  explain any such retention plainly.
- **Marketing opt-out:** honored promptly and does not affect essential
  service messages such as order or security notifications.

## Verification And Safety
Always verify identity before discussing or changing any personal data, using
the account recovery standards. Never confirm whether an account exists to an
unverified caller, and never send personal data to an unverified address.

## Incidents
Any suspected exposure or unauthorized access to personal data is a P1 under
the escalation matrix: route immediately to the security queue and avoid
speculative statements to the customer until the incident lead responds.

## Agent Steps
1. Verify identity.
2. Identify the specific right being exercised.
3. Log the request and route deletion or access requests to the privacy
   workflow with the statutory deadline noted.
4. Confirm the action and timeline in writing.

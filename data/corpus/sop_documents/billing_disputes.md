# Billing Disputes

## Scope
Use this when a customer questions a charge: an amount they do not recognize,
a charge larger than expected, a duplicate charge, or a charge after they
believed a service had ended. Refund eligibility itself is governed by the
refund policy; this procedure focuses on investigating the charge.

## Triage
1. Identify the exact charge: date, amount, and descriptor.
2. Match it to an order, subscription renewal, tax/shipping component, or
   pre-authorization hold.
3. Distinguish a true duplicate from a pending authorization that will drop
   off automatically within 3–5 business days.

## Common Causes
- **Pre-authorization holds:** temporary and release on their own; explain
  rather than refund.
- **Proration:** plan changes mid-cycle create a partial line item.
- **Tax or shipping:** shown separately from the item subtotal.
- **Genuine duplicate or system error:** reverse promptly and prioritize.

## Resolution
Confirmed billing errors and duplicates are corrected and refunded to the
original payment method within 3–5 business days. If the customer initiated a
chargeback with their bank, do not also issue a manual refund — coordinate
through the payments team to avoid a double credit.

## Agent Steps
1. Verify identity per account recovery standards before discussing charges.
2. Explain the charge clearly or open a correction.
3. Provide a reference number and the expected resolution timeline.
4. Log the dispute reason for financial reporting.

## Escalation
Disputed amounts above standard agent authority, chargeback conflicts, or
suspected fraud route to the billing supervisor via the escalation matrix.

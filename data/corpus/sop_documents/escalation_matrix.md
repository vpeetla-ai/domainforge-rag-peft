# Escalation Matrix

## Purpose
Defines when and how to move a case beyond a first-line agent so customers
get the right authority quickly without unnecessary hand-offs.

## Severity Levels
- **P1 — Critical:** confirmed account takeover, payment fraud, data breach,
  or a widespread outage. Escalate immediately to the security or incident
  queue; do not wait for the customer to follow up.
- **P2 — High:** data loss risk, repeated failed resolution, high-value
  dispute, legal or regulatory mention, or a vulnerable customer. Route to a
  supervisor the same business day.
- **P3 — Medium:** standard dispute above agent authority, policy exception
  request, or a reproducible technical bug. Queue to the relevant specialist
  team.
- **P4 — Low:** routine question answerable with existing procedures. No
  escalation.

## Authority Limits
First-line agents may approve refunds, goodwill credits, and shipping waivers
up to the standard threshold. Amounts above the threshold, policy exceptions,
and chargeback conflicts require supervisor approval.

## Routing
- Billing and payment conflicts → billing supervisor.
- Account takeover and fraud → security queue.
- Reproducible bugs and data loss → engineering.
- Legal, press, or regulatory mentions → escalations lead, no substantive
  reply until cleared.

## Handing Off
Include a concise summary, verified identity status, actions already taken,
and the desired outcome. Tell the customer who will follow up and by when.
Never close a case as resolved while an escalation is still open.
